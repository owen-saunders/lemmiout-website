Box-Car Racers

Moving with current times and the new curriculum. we are offering a STEM based activity project to schools. Our aim is to offer exciting but educational activities that can be delivered with an outdoor/active focus.

We have put together a project for pupils to complete that is customisable. It will take a number of hours (depending on delivery requirements by the school).

Planning and Design

Our project “boxcar racers” consists of a digital workbook which can be completed either on a computer or printed and written on.

The workbook aims to start a design engineering process where a pupil works to create a car body. As they progress, they will research vehicle design, sketch ideas, build scale models and work both independently as well as with a partner, to eventually critique and consolidate ideas as a group of six.

Team Building

The workbook is supported by Lesson guidance notes which include additional side projects to develop understanding of key areas such as aerodynamics.

As the project progresses the design teams will use the cardboard provided to increase the scale of their designs to construct their final idea which will be large enough for the pupils to sit in.

Encouraged by the booklet they will then research an ethical company, so that they can write slogans and logos which they will then decorate their racer with as a “sponsor” would a race car.

We will then visit your site and host a race day with your class, where the pupils will construct our unique car chassis and steering mechanism onto which they will attach their prepared cardboard bodywork.

Evaluating

After completing our big and exciting race day, following the final stages of the booklet, pupils will complete an evaluation exercise and review using media and technology to record their experience.

Presenting and Awards

The project is then completed by the team writing a pitch which they will deliver to the year group below which will bolster evidence to achieve standards which we replicate from STEM to allow us to decide whether your class achieves bronze, silver or gold standard in the boxcar awards.

And it's all...

The entire project is designed to be sustainable and environmentally friendly with all elements used being reusable or recyclable, activity focused and encouraging engagement, participation and a sense of competitive education.
